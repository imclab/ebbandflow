About

Daniel grew up in Minneapolis, Minnesota, and attended the University of Colorado, in Boulder. He began as a film maker in Boulder, studying experimental films under Stan Brakhage and Phil Solomon. He became established in the San Francisco film community, producing documentaries and exhibiting his experimental films at local venues such as Artists Television Access, the San Francisco Cinematheque, Yerba Buena Center for the Arts, and distributing his work through Canyon Cinema. His films have been screened internationally at venues such as the Centre Pompidou in Paris, the Austrian Film Museum, the Museum of Latin American Art in Buenos Aires, the Strasbourg, France, the Institute of Contemporary Art, Boston, as well as venues in Helsinki, Finland, and Seoul, South Korea.

In recent years, Baker has expanded into the world of fabrication, photography, mixed media, projection design, and computer programming. His work contemplates organic biological design, death, decay, and the intersection of the natural world with human detritus and technology.
